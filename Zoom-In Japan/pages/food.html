😋SUSHI

SUSHI refers to "rice seasonesd with sweetened vinegar" and are often topped and filled with varitey ingredients.

Sushi surprisingly wasn't originated from "Japan" but from ""China". It's all begun with a dish known as "Narezushi", a salted fish wrapped in fermented rice. The way it was preserved, prevented it from spoiling. The technique soon spread to Japan. Later on, the Japanese enhanced the flavor with seasoned rice. However, not until Edo period (1603-1868), fresh fish was served with seasoned rice and seaweed, which evolved to the today mordern sushi.

😅FUGU aka. PUFFER FISH

Yes,PUFFER FISH is 😨"poisonous" 😨 but people in Japan still eat it anyway because it tastes heavenly (no pun intended) and very nutritious. They can only be served at restaurants in Japan with qualified fugu handlers. I'm dedicated the whole section just for Fugu since it is so interesting to know about.

Source from Web-Japan.org

Click to Englarge

FUGU

FUGU's poison parts are removed and washed thoroughly by handlers. Then, carefully slice and lay out like flowers on a platter. This process is very time consuming. This is why Fugu considers a luxury type of fish.

Click to Englarge

FUGU continued...

There are many way to eat Fugu. It's can be servered in hot pot and deep-fried tempura style. The skin is not to be thrown away, it also got eaten after lightly boiled and is dipped in a special sauce called Ponzu.

Click to Englarge

FUGU ROE

FUGU ROEcan also be fermented in rice bran. The egg sacs of the fish are pickled for three years to remove poison. Why this process removes the poison, it is still a mystery till today.

RAMEN

RAMEN is one of the most popular
Japanese dish beside sushi. Though, it is originated from China and brought over to Japan in early 1900s.There are well-known miso ramen, tonkotsu (pork bone) ramen, shoyu (soysauce) ramen, chicken ramen and tsukemen (noodles with dipping sauce). It's often served with Japnese roasted porkbelly, karagi (fried chicken) and soft boild eggs.

Click to Englarge

SOBA

SOBA are made from buckwheat. They are chewy and served cold with tsuyu diping sauce along with green onions, chopped nori (seaweed) and wasabi. The two main ways to eat it are zaru soba (served cold) or kake soba (served hot in broth).

Click to Englarge

UDON

UDON are well known for their thickness
and soft, chewy texture. Udon also inherited from China. It is served in both hot and cold. Hot udon is served in a dashi (fish stock)and soysauce broth called kakejiru, or along with a Japese curry dipping broth. Cold udon with light broth is served usually in summer.
