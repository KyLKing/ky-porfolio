ORIGAMI

Origami was initially known as orikata (folded shapes). In 1880, however, the craft became known as origami. The term origami comes from the Japanese words oru (to fold) and kami (paper).
Although origami is typically associated with Japan, it became popular in Europe, too.

Source from LIVE JAPAN

The First Known Book of Origami

"The Secret to Folding One-thousand Cranes"(Hiden Senbazuru Orikata), was published in 1797 and contains detailed diagrams for dozens of models, including the iconic paper crane (tsuru).

Modular Origami

Modular means "many" or "multiple". This means that for this type of origami, it will use more than one piece of paper. Each piece is identical to each other for a certain model, such as 3D affect.

Wet-Folding Origami

Wet-folding is an origami technique created by Akira Yoshizawa (a grand master of origami) that uses water to dampen the paper so that it can be manipulated more easily.

MARTIAL ARTS

With the rise of warrior class in the late twelfth century, the bushi or samurai (members of the warrior class) trained in disciplines as kenjutsu (sword art), iaijutsu (sword-drawing art), jujutsu (unarmed combat), kyujutsu (Japanese marchery), sojutsu (spear art),bajutsu(horsemanship), and suijutsu (swimming). These gradually became standardized into styles or schools during Edo period (1603-1867).

After Meiji Restoration in 1868, the warrior class disappeared; and the adoption of mordern weaponry participate in some of these arts declined.

In the 1895, a national organization called Dao Nippon Budo Kai (The Great Japan Martial Arts Association) centralized into the educational system.

JUDO

JUDO means the gentle way, developed from an older art known as jujutsu, which generally is avoided using weapons. Kano Jigoro (1860-1938), formulated a training system based on modern athletic principles and modified the rules to permit both throwing and grappling on the map. Judo doesn't permit striking or kicking opponent like Karate does.

Click to Enlarge

AIKIDO

AIKIDO has its origins in Aiki jujutsu of the Daito school of jujutsu, founded by Minamoto Yoshimitsu (1045-1127). Ueshiba Morihei (1883-1969) is credited with the developing aikido into its present form.

Aikido contestants do not grasp each other's collars and sleeves like Judo does but rather stay apart from each other. Aikido techniques mainly seek to take advantage of an opponent's weakness in wrist and arm joints. It emphasis on self defense, which is the reason why it is popular among women and those who are in law enforcement.

KARATE

KARATEDO, which means the way of empty hand, developed over 1,000 years ago in China. It was introduced to the island of Okinawa many centuries ago as a form of weaponless self defense.

In Karate, every part of the body can be turned into a fighting weapon. At the same time, using defensive techniques to sidestep or block an opponent's thrusts and kicks.

KENDO

KENDO is Japanese stick fencing. It's emphasized the sword art of samurai. In 1952, with the establishment of All Japan Kendo Federation, kendo was revived as a sport and was introduced into curricula of junior high and high shcools.

In Kendo, points are scored by strking the opponent's head, trunk, or wrist, or jabbing the throat.

KYUDO

KYUDO (Japnese Archery) developed during Japan's feudal period as a fighting art. With the fouding of Japan Kyudo Federation, in 1949, the discipline made a new start as a sport.

In contest, each competitor usually shoots arrows at a target 28 to 60 meters away. The bow, which is about 2.21 meters long, is made of wood and bamboo. Who hits the target with the greatest number of arrows wins the match. Unlike Western archery, kyudo emphasizes the importance of form.

TEA

Sado( Japanese green tea ceremony) is an ancient tradition with roots traced back to Zen Buddhism. Literally translated as "the way of the tea", the ceremony involves the preparation and drinking of tea. Sado requires years of study and perfection to master. Sado is widely taught in schools across Japan and still remains a popular hobby today.
